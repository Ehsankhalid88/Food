Mote Hin Gar : National Dish of Myanmar (Burma)

For some nations, the national dish offers nothing more than hollow symbolism for the cuisine as a whole. For others, however, the national dish holds a far greater significance and lends a far deeper meaning to the overall national identity. The country of Myanmar (formerly Burma) falls into this latter category. For a once proud nation ransacked by intergroup conflicts and civil war, just about anything that brings popular common consensus is a welcome reprieve. The delightful dish of mohinga is one such reprieve.

Some information about Mohinga

Mohinga is quite a dish like we have rarely seen before. It is a noodle soup. This noodle soup is considered the Burmese national dish and is eaten for breakfast, lunch and dinner. Some eats for breakfast dish (and what a delightful breakfast it would make!) while some enjoys at any part of the day. If you’re craving for some mohinga in the middle of the afternoon in downtown Yangon, chances are good you’ll quickly find a street vendor to serve you a fresh batch.
